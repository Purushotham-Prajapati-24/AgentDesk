The AI support agent market consolidated in 2025–2026 around a handful of platforms that each carved out a niche. If you're evaluating tools right now, the field is small enough to compare directly — and the differences between them are now sharp enough to matter.

We compared the five leading platforms on the dimensions that actually affect a support team: RAG grounding quality, human handoff depth, embed flexibility, pricing, and total cost of ownership. Here's how they stack up.

## The shortlist

| Tool | Best for | Starting price | Human handoff | RAG | Embed options |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **AgentDesk** | Handoff + open-source + tenant isolation | Free tier + usage credits | Native (full context) | Qdrant + hybrid | Script, iframe, React, Vue |
| **Chatbase** | Fast, simple deployment | $19/mo | Limited (via integrations) | Good | Script, iframe |

> **How we evaluated:** RAG quality = retrieval accuracy + grounding (does it cite sources, does it hallucinate). Handoff depth = does the operator get the full transcript and session context, or just a forwarded message? Embed flexibility = how many ways can you install the widget. Pricing = real total cost at scale, not just the entry tier.

## 1. AgentDesk — best for human handoff + open-source

AgentDesk is the platform built around the premise that an AI support agent is valuable *because it knows when to stop*. Where competitors optimize for deflection (the percentage of tickets the bot closes alone), AgentDesk optimizes for the handoff — the moment the bot escalates to a human with full context preserved.

**Strengths:**
- **Native human handoff.** When the bot escalates, the full transcript, session token, and tenant scope move to a live operator inbox. The operator replies on the same chat surface — no channel switch, no context loss.
- **Tenant isolation.** Each tenant's documents, widget config, and conversations are scoped separately. No cross-tenant retrieval. Critical for B2B and agencies.
- **Embed flexibility.** Script (any site), iframe (inline panel), React/Next.js SDK, Vue SDK — four install paths.
- **Open-source repository.** You can inspect, self-host, or extend the codebase. No vendor lock-in.
- **Hybrid search.** Qdrant powers both vector and keyword retrieval, so exact-match queries (order numbers, SKUs) work alongside semantic search.

**Weaknesses:**
- Newer ecosystem — fewer pre-built integrations than Chatbase.
- Documentation is still maturing (we're fixing that — see the [developer docs](/docs)).

**Best for:** Teams that need credible escalation, B2B/agency setups requiring tenant isolation, and developers who want an open, inspectable platform.

## 2. Chatbase — best for fast, simple deployment

Chatbase is the market leader by volume. If you want a chatbot live on your site in 10 minutes with minimal configuration, Chatbase delivers.

**Strengths:**
- Fastest time-to-live in the category.
- Large library of integrations (Slack, Zapier, Notion, etc.).
- Polished dashboard — non-technical users can configure it.
- Strong brand recognition and comparison content (they own the "Chatbase alternative" SERP).

**Weaknesses:**
- Human handoff is limited and routed through third-party integrations, not a native inbox.
- No tenant isolation — multi-tenant setups require workarounds.
- Closed-source; no self-host option.

**Best for:** Small teams that want a chatbot live today and don't need complex escalation. [Chatbase vs AgentDesk comparison →](/alternatives/chatbase)

## 3. DocsBot — best for document-heavy teams

DocsBot positioned itself around "train AI on your docs" and has strong retrieval quality when the knowledge base is well-structured.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent document ingestion and chunking.
- Strong retrieval accuracy on technical content.
- Good developer documentation.

**Weaknesses:**
- No native operator inbox — handoff is a gap.
- Slower to ship AI-citation features (Perplexity, ChatGPT visibility).
- Marketing content is thin relative to Chatbase.

**Best for:** Internal knowledge bases and dev-tooling teams whose primary need is document Q&A. [DocsBot vs AgentDesk comparison →](/alternatives/docsbot)

## 4. SiteGPT — best for non-technical site owners

SiteGPT targets website owners who want a chatbot without touching code. The setup is wizard-driven and the positioning is vertical (real estate, SaaS, agencies).

**Strengths:**
- Easiest no-code setup in the category.
- Vertical landing pages for specific industries.
- Good for site owners who never want to see a script tag.

**Weaknesses:**
- Generic positioning — weak technical differentiation.
- No human handoff.
- Limited embed options (script and iframe only).
- Weakest technical SEO of the top five.

**Best for:** Non-technical site owners in vertical markets who want a "set and forget" chatbot. [SiteGPT vs AgentDesk comparison →](/alternatives/sitegpt)

## 5. Intercom Fin — best for enterprise Intercom users

Fin is Intercom's AI agent. If you're already on Intercom, Fin is the path of least resistance — it's native to the workflow you already use.

**Strengths:**
- Deepest integration with an existing support stack (for Intercom customers).
- Strong retrieval quality backed by Intercom's engineering.
- Enterprise-grade compliance and support.

**Weaknesses:**
- $0.99 per resolution pricing is the highest in the category — it scales expensively.
- Assumes you're on Intercom. If you're not, the switching cost is enormous.
- No script/iframe embed for non-Intercom surfaces.

**Best for:** Existing Intercom customers who want AI without leaving the platform. For everyone else, the alternatives are dramatically cheaper.

## How to choose

Use this decision tree:

1. **Already on Intercom and happy with it?** → Intercom Fin. The integration premium is worth it.
2. **Need credible human handoff with context preservation?** → AgentDesk. It's the only one built around this.
3. **Want live in 10 minutes, no code, simple use case?** → Chatbase.
4. **Internal knowledge base, document Q&A focus?** → DocsBot.
5. **Non-technical site owner, vertical market?** → SiteGPT.
6. **Multi-tenant or B2B with isolation requirements?** → AgentDesk. Tenant isolation is a first-class feature, not a workaround.
